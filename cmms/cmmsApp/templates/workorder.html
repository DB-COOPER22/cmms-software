What is a Work Order?

A Work Order (WO) is an authorized instruction to perform a maintenance task on an asset, location, or system. It defines what must be done, why it’s needed, who will do it, when it’s due, and the resources (labor, parts, tools) required. In CARL, WOs provide full traceability from request to close-out, with costs, evidence, and approvals captured along the way.

Lifecycle of a Work Order

Work Order

For Technicians & Stores

Request : Issue reported or task generated (manual, PM, IoT/condition, meter, inspection). Review & Approve –Screening, priority/SLA set, safety checks, and role-based approval.
Plan: Scope the job: steps/checklist, parts, tools, permits, skills, estimates. Schedule & Assign: Allocate teams/technicians, align with availability and site windows.
Execute – Technician completes tasks, captures readings, photos, signatures (online/offline). Close – Record cause/remedy codes, time, parts, and final costs; verify quality.
Analyse – Review KPIs (MTBF/MTTR), SLA performance, and improvement actions.

What’s in CARL Work Plans

Preventive Maintenance (PM)

Planned work performed at time or meter intervals to prevent failures.

  • When to use: For critical assets where failure risk/cost is high; OEM-recommended tasks.

  • Carl Support:
    • Time/meter/seasonal PM plans with auto WO generation.
    • Reusable job plans, checklists, and required parts lists.
    • Calendar & crew scheduling; workload levelling across sites.
    • Compliance evidence (photos, readings, signatures) and audit trail.

Corrective Maintenance (CM)

Reactive work to fix a detected fault or restore function after a failure.

  • When to use: Non-planned repairs, breakdowns, safety incidents, inspection findings.

  • Carl Support:
    • Fast WO creation from requests, inspections, or mobile app.
    • Priorities & SLAs, auto-notifications, and escalation rules.
    • Parts availability checks and substitute suggestions from stores.
    • Root-cause & remedy codes; cost capture (labor/subcontract/parts).

Predictive Maintenance (PdM)

Condition-based interventions triggered by sensor trends, thresholds, or analytics.

  • When to use: For assets where data (vibration, temperature, power, oil) is available.

  • Carl Support:
    • IoT/SCADA integrations to raise condition alerts and WOs automatically.
    • Meter points & limits; trend views embedded in the WO.
    • Risk-based prioritisation and SLA alignment for emerging issues.
    • Feedback loop: outcomes feed future thresholds and PM optimization.
